Introduction

LearnLi, as an academic platform, upholds the highest standards of content integrity, educational value, and compliance with international academic and online publishing regulations. To maintain the trust of our users and ensure a safe, inclusive, and enriching learning environment, a robust content moderation system is vital.

Guiding Principles

  • Educational Integrity: Content should align with educational purposes and learning objectives.
  • Inclusivity and Respect: Content must respect all users regardless of race, religion, gender, or background.
  • Compliance with Laws: Courses must adhere to international laws regarding copyright, intellectual property, and privacy.
  • Transparency: Moderation decisions should be clear, consistent, and justifiable.
  • Escalation for Ambiguities: Moderators must escalate cases where violations are unclear or borderline.

Flaggable and Inappropriate Content

Categories of Flaggable Content

  • Harmful or Offensive Content: Hate speech, graphic violence, harassment, etc.
  • Academic Violations: Plagiarism, inaccurate content, or dishonesty-promoting material.
  • Misinformation: Courses spreading unverified or false information.
  • Intellectual Property Infringement: Unauthorized use of copyrighted material.
  • Technical Violations: Broken files, incomplete uploads, or technical errors.

Degrees of Inappropriateness

Low Degree: Minor issues such as typos. Action: Approve with corrections.

Moderate Degree: Ambiguous content requiring expert judgment. Action: Escalate for review.

High Degree: Severe violations like harmful or illegal content. Action: Immediate rejection.

Case Assignments and Procedures

Courses Academic Experts + Content Reviewers 48 hours

Escalation Protocol

Moderators must provide screenshots, detailed descriptions, and links to policies when escalating flagged content to the reviewer panel, which includes senior moderators, academic experts, and legal advisors.

Moderator Conduct and Compliance

Moderators are bound by LearnLi’s Confidentiality Agreement and must adhere to legal and ethical standards, including periodic training.
