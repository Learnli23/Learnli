Introduction

eBooks are a significant part of the LearnLi platform, offering a structured, text-based learning experience. To maintain quality and compliance while protecting user integrity, this policy document provides guidelines for moderating uploaded eBooks. Moderation ensures that eBooks meet LearnLi’s educational and ethical standards, align with legal requirements, and foster a safe and inclusive digital learning environment. These guidelines define inappropriate content, establish criteria for flagged content, and detail procedures for approval, rejection, and escalation of flagged eBooks

Guiding Principles

  • Educational Vlaue: Ensure eBooks contribute to LearnLi's mission of providing high-quality educational resources.
  • Compliance: Adhere to intellectual property laws, copyright standards, and data privacy regulations.
  • Inclusivity: Avoid discriminatory or offensive content.
  • Accuracy: Promote factual and verifiable information.
  • TransparencyMaintain clear, fair, and consistent moderation procedures.

Flaggable and Inappropriate Blog Content

Categories of Flaggable Content

  • Harmful or Offensive Content:Hate speech, discriminatory language, or inflammatory remarks,Explicit sexual content, excessive violence, or abuse.
  • Academic Misconduct:uncredited use of intellectual property,Plagiarism of other authors’ works, False or misleading educational claims..
  • Misinformation and Inaccuracy:Incorrect, misleading, or unverifiable information in critical areas (e.g., medicine, science, finance). Spam or Non-Educational Content:
  • eBooks with promotional or irrelevant content. Irrelevant promotional content or phishing links,Material solely intended for advertising purposes
  • Non-Compliant Content: Privacy law violations or breaches of copyright, Content violating intellectual property or copyright laws. Inclusion of sensitive personal data or private information.
  • Misleading Structure or TitlesTitles that do not reflect the actual content. Clickbait tactics.

Degrees of Inappropriateness

Low Degree (Minor Issues):Minor typographical errors or formatting inconsistencies. Slightly outdated references.
Action:Ignore but recommend edits.

Moderate Degree (Borderline Inappropriate):Misleading but not outright harmful information. Incomplete or underdeveloped content.
Action: Escalate for further review.

High Degree (Severe Violations):Explicitly offensive, harmful, or illegal content. Plagiarized or heavily inaccurate information.
Action: Reject and remove immediately.

Procedures for Moderation Actions

Ignore of Flagged Lessons

When to Ignore:

    Flagged content does not meet inappropriate criteria. Errors are minor and do not affect the eBook's quality or educational value. Steps: Approve the eBook and notify the flagging user of the decision. Provide optional recommendations to the content creator for improvements. Rejection of Flagged eBooks When to Reject: Content violates LearnLi’s policies or laws. Content is harmful, inappropriate, or academically unsound. Steps: Reject and remove the eBook. Notify the content creator with a detailed explanation. Allow for an appeal if applicable. Escalation of Flagged eBooks When to Escalate: Ambiguous cases requiring expert input. Legal or ethical concerns requiring higher-level review. Steps: Document the flagged content and provide detailed notes for reviewers. Notify the flagger and the content creator of the escalation status.
